Project Overview

Aurox equips advanced traders with automation, analytics, and complete transparency. As the user base grew past 5M, the platform needed a wallet tightly connected to the terminal so people could reach their assets instantly from any device.

The wallet had to feel trusted for institutional desks yet approachable for first-time crypto investors.

Goals

We aligned on measurable targets for the launch:

  • Integrate the wallet directly into the Aurox trading terminal.
  • Deliver parity across desktop, tablet, and mobile without extra friction.
  • Ensure users can access, transfer, and secure assets in seconds.

Solution

I partnered with engineering and marketing leaders to define the interaction model, information hierarchy, and risk posture for every flow.

High-fidelity prototypes unlocked feedback from support, growth, and VIP trading desks so we could tune onboarding, staking, and alert levels before code.

We created a Web 2.0-inspired key recovery path so newcomers could secure their wallets without fear of permanent lockouts.

Lead Designer Role

As lead designer I synchronized flows across wallet, terminal, and marketing demo environments. I ran critiques, set research cadences, and facilitated stakeholder reviews across time zones.

I also produced handoff guidelines, shaped the backlog, and opened the system so motion and brand partners could contribute safely.

Impact

The refreshed wallet narrative lifted sales demos and made enterprise prospects more confident in the roadmap.

  • Users praised the cross-device accessibility and transparent status updates.
  • Support tickets about syncing and access dropped after launch.
  • The new design system powers ongoing roadmap work, speeding future releases.

The project ultimately increased engagement, time-on-task, and the quality of investor conversations.
